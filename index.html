Legal & Government Information

Important Disclosures & Legal Information:

  • Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA): You have the right to dispute inaccurate information on your credit report. Credit bureaus must investigate disputes within 30 days.
  • Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA): Debt collectors cannot harass you, use false statements, or engage in unfair practices when collecting debts.
  • Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA): Credit repair companies cannot make false claims, charge fees before services are rendered, or guarantee specific results.
  • Free Annual Credit Reports: You are entitled to one free credit report every 12 months from each of the three major credit bureaus through AnnualCreditReport.com.
  • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB): The CFPB provides resources and accepts complaints about credit reporting issues and credit repair services.
  • Federal Trade Commission (FTC): The FTC enforces consumer protection laws and provides information about credit repair rights and scams to avoid.

Government Resources:

  • AnnualCreditReport.com - The ONLY authorized source for free annual credit reports
  • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau - www.consumerfinance.gov
  • Federal Trade Commission - www.ftc.gov
  • Office of the Comptroller of the Currency - www.occ.gov

Disclaimer: This service helps you exercise your legal rights under federal credit laws. We do not guarantee specific results, and we cannot remove accurate negative information from your credit report. Results may vary based on individual circumstances.

Cold Call Scripts

Professional scripts for credit repair sales calls. Use these to guide your conversations with potential clients.

Introduction Script

"Hi [Prospect Name], this is [Your Name] from [Your Company Name]. How are you doing today? The reason for my call is I wanted to share some important information about your credit rights and how we're helping people in [City/State] improve their credit scores. Many people don't realize they have specific rights under federal law that allow them to challenge inaccurate information on their credit reports. We specialize in exercising those rights to help people remove errors and improve their credit scores. I noticed you might be interested in improving your credit situation. Would you be open to a quick, no-obligation consultation to see how we could potentially help you?"
Key Points to Emphasize:
  • Be confident and professional
  • Focus on education and rights, not pressure
  • Keep it conversational, not scripted
  • Listen more than you talk
  • Ask open-ended questions

Overcoming Objections Script

Objection: "I can't afford it right now." Response: "I completely understand budget concerns. Many of our clients felt the same way initially. However, they found that improving their credit actually saved them money in the long run through better interest rates and opportunities. We offer flexible payment plans, and the cost is often much less than what people are paying in higher interest rates due to their current credit situation." Objection: "I've tried credit repair before and it didn't work." Response: "I appreciate you sharing that. Not all credit repair services are the same. Many companies use generic, automated systems. We use a personalized approach with certified credit consultants who understand the specific laws and regulations. We also provide complete transparency throughout the process and only work on items that are actually disputable under the law." Objection: "I need to think about it." Response: "That's completely understandable. Credit decisions are important. Can I ask what specific concerns you'd like to think about? Often, I can address those questions right now. Also, I should mention that we offer a free credit report analysis with no obligation, which might help with your decision-making process."
Handling Objections:
  • Listen fully before responding
  • Acknowledge their concern
  • Provide value-based responses
  • Ask clarifying questions
  • Transition back to benefits

Closing Script

"Based on our conversation, it sounds like improving your credit could really help you [mention specific benefit they discussed, like: qualify for better mortgage rates, save on auto loans, get approved for that business loan, etc.]. Here's what I recommend as our next step: We'll schedule your free credit report analysis for [specific day/time]. During this 20-minute consultation, we'll: 1. Review your current credit situation 2. Identify specific items we can potentially address 3. Explain exactly how our process works 4. Answer all your questions 5. If it makes sense for you, we can discuss moving forward No pressure, no obligation - just valuable information to help you make an informed decision. Does [suggested time] work for you, or would [alternative time] be better?"
Closing Tips:
  • Summarize benefits they care about
  • Make the next step easy and clear
  • Give two time options (this or that)
  • Confirm details and follow up
  • Send confirmation immediately

Follow-up Script

"Hi [Prospect Name], it's [Your Name] from [Your Company Name]. We spoke [time period] ago about credit repair options. I wanted to follow up because I was reviewing some notes from our conversation and remembered you mentioned [specific concern or goal they shared]. I was thinking about your situation and wanted to share that we recently helped someone in a similar situation [brief success story if appropriate]. I know you were considering your options, and I wanted to check if you had any additional questions or if there's anything specific I can clarify about how our service works? Also, I wanted to let you know that we're currently [mention any current offer, limited time opportunity, or special program if applicable]." Alternative for previous consultation: "Hi [Prospect Name], following up on our credit analysis consultation. I wanted to see if you had any thoughts or questions after having some time to review the information we discussed. Sometimes people think of additional questions after our initial conversation, and I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for your situation."
Follow-up Strategy:
  • Reference previous conversation specifics
  • Provide additional value or information
  • Be helpful, not pushy
  • Have a specific reason for following up
  • Listen for timing or readiness signals
